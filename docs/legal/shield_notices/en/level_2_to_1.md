# You are lowering the protection level to Free Flow (Level 1)

## This is a controls REDUCTION. Read carefully.

By leaving Level 2 (Compliance) you are **turning off segregation of duties** on formal actions.

## What remains active at Level 1

- DLP blocking prompts and responses with sensitive data.
- Automatic cost monitoring and spending caps.
- Full audit trail of every interaction with immutable records.
- Policy denials enforced automatically.

## What is switched off

- **Segregation of duties**: policy publication, risk assessments, and exception approvals will now allow the same person to request and approve their own action.
- **SOC 2 non-compliance** for the controls that depend on SoD (CC6.1, CC7.2, CC8.1). Organizations pursuing SOC 2 Type II should not operate at this level for those specific controls.

## Audit posture impact

- Any internal process that depends on a different-approver will need to be re-examined.
- If your organization is audited (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.), formally record this decision and its rationale.
- Auditors reviewing this evidence record will see the explicit controls reduction.

## This change is recorded

On confirmation, your identity, the exact timestamp, and the text of this notice will be recorded in an immutable platform event. This record makes the organizational decision to reduce controls explicit.
